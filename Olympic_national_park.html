BACKPACKING
With 611 miles of trails in the park, and nearly one million acres of wilderness to explore. From rugged, glaciated peaks, to isolated coastlines, the only American rainforests, high alpine lakes and amazing waterfalls, the trails of Olympic lead to some of the best destinations in the world.  
BOATING
With Rivers, lakes, and coasts, Olympic has a place for paddlers and motor boat enthusiasts. With 73 miles of wilderness coast, 3,000 miles of rivers and streams to explore.
CAMPING
Camping in Olympic lets you experience several different Olympic Peninsula landscapes. Plan your camping trip and stay at one of Olympic's 16 campgrounds.
FISHING
Fishermen can enjoy their favorite pastime in all seasons at Olympic. With 4,000 miles of fishable waters, 600 high country lakes, and 70 miles of tidewater habitats, Olympic National Park offers excellent fishing for 31 species of fish.
WILDLIFE
VIEWING
Interested in encountering wildlife in the wilderness? Learn about the best places to see animals in the park. Many wild animals dwell within Olympic National Park. Despite their abundance, viewing wildlife is often a matter of luck and diligence.
